About Raksha Bandhan

Raksha Bandhan, also known as Rakhi, is a cherished Indian festival that celebrates the eternal bond of love between a brother and a sister. The name "Raksha Bandhan" translates to "the bond of protection, obligation, or care."

On this auspicious day, a sister ties a sacred thread, the Rakhi, on her brother's wrist. This thread symbolizes her love and prayers for his well-being, and in return, the brother promises to protect and support her throughout her life.

The festival transcends biological relationships and is also celebrated between cousins and friends, reinforcing the values of love, care, and harmony in society.

The beauty of Raksha Bandhan lies in its inclusivity. It is not limited to biological siblings alone; it extends to cousins, close friends, and even neighbors, highlighting the festival‚Äôs broader message of unity, respect, and harmony within society.
